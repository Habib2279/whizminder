ISO 9000 FAQ

What's the difference between ISO 9000 and ISO 9001?

ISO 9000 is a family of standards that includes ISO 9001 (the requirements standard), ISO 9000 (fundamentals and vocabulary), and ISO 9004 (guidance for performance improvement). ISO 9001 is the standard organizations get certified against.

How long does QMS certification last?

ISO 9001 certification is valid for 3 years, with surveillance audits typically conducted annually. After 3 years, a recertification audit is required to maintain certification.

Can small businesses benefit from ISO 9000?

Absolutely. ISO 9001 can be scaled to any organization size. For small businesses, it provides structure for consistent quality, helps win new business, and often reveals efficiency improvements that save money.

What industries use ISO 9000?

ISO 9001 is industry-neutral and used worldwide across manufacturing, services, healthcare, government, and non-profits. Some industries have sector-specific versions (e.g., AS9100 for aerospace, IATF 16949 for automotive).
